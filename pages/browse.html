FERN

A fern plant is a primitive, non-flowering vascular plant that reproduces through spores. It typically has long, delicate fronds that unfurl from coiled structures known as fiddleheads. Ferns are found in a variety of habitats, from moist forests to tropical jungles and even arid regions. They are known for their graceful appearance and are admired for their ability to add a touch of natural elegance to landscapes and indoor settings.

WILLOW

A willow plant is a deciduous tree or shrub belonging to the Salix genus. It is commonly found in temperate regions, growing near water bodies like rivers, streams, and wetlands. Willows are recognized for their graceful, pendulous branches that often sweep downward, giving them a distinctive appearance. They have slender, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in catkins during spring.
